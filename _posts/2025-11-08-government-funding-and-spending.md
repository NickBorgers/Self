This is a collection of links for better understanding how the USA government brings in revenue and spends money.

* [Revenues and expenditures](https://usafacts.org/government-spending/)
    * Most complete and detailed, but lots of information onscreen - may be overwhelming
* [Expenditures](https://www.usaspending.gov/explorer/budget_function)
    * Block shaped drill down, no revenue data but easy to consume
* [Expenditures](https://fiscaldata.treasury.gov/americas-finance-guide/federal-spending/)
    * If you prefer a "news article" structure
* [Federal Taxpayer Receipt](https://us.abalancingact.com/federal-taxpayer-receipt)
    * You do NOT include your payroll taxes in this, only federal income taxes which excludes FICA
    * For [someone making $100,000 per year they pay $13,841 in federal income taxes](https://smartasset.com/taxes/income-taxes#byYkBLaTqb)
    * [Someone earning $400,000 pays $105,264](https://smartasset.com/taxes/income-taxes#MOs452Iy1K)
